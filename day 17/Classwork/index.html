About Pandas

Pandas are large, bear-like mammals that are native to the mountain forests of southwest China. They are easily recognized by their distinct black-and-white coloring and are loved worldwide. Pandas mainly eat bamboo and spend most of their time foraging and eating. Despite being classified as carnivores, their diet is over 99% bamboo.

Pandas are solitary animals and are known for their playful behavior. They are a symbol of wildlife conservation and are classified as vulnerable due to habitat loss and low birth rates in the wild.
